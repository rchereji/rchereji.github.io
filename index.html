Research Interests
An unsolved puzzle of Biophysics is how a human cell is able to pack 6 feet of DNA within the narrow confines of the cell nucleus, which is only 1 ten-thousandth of an inch across, while keeping the DNA accessible for replication, transcription, and other functions. Nucleosomes are the basic units of DNA packaging, and DNA wrapped into nucleosomes is hindered from interacting with many DNA-binding proteins. Understanding nucleosome positioning is thus important for the understanding of promoter accessibility and transcription regulation. I study physical models which describe the chromatin organization, its dynamics and the interactions between DNA and DNA-binding proteins, including histones, transcription factors, polymerases and chromatin remodelers.
